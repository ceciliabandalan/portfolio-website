Inquizi

Objective: Rapidly design a functional prototype using the design process

Problem Statement: How might we design a mobile app that empowers people to learn new vocabulary?

The Product: This mobile app aims to provide students with an efficient way of learning new vocabulary, through customizable ways of creating and studying from flashcards. Inquizi is designed for the overwhelmed, full-time student learning new terms, concepts, and ideas from multiple subjects

Role: UX Researcher, UX Designer, Visual Designer

My Process

Research

Competitor Analysis

I chose three established language learning apps with the goal of exploring pros and cons of existing designs + evaluating how I feel using each product as a way to uncover possible solutions to improve

Key Findings: Duolingo was the most successful in providing a delightful learning experience through the use of quick fun games. Rosetta Stone was the most effective in getting the user to cognitively think, which is perfect for the serious learner

User Interviews

Research Goals: identify studying pains, needs, motivations, attitudes, and potential solutions

Criteria: adults enrolled in university, tech-savvy

Method: 3 participants, 30 mins each

Key Findings:

  • Each interviewee had a different approach when it came to retaining information, which suggests that effective studying is unique to each individual
  • Similarities found with each interviewee is the repetitive practice. Students will often review their notes multiple times throughout the week to successfully retain information
Through my research findings, I am confident that a customizable studying solution may be the best fit.

Ideation

User Personas

To further empathize with my user, I created a persona based on the information I had collected from my research. This will help me advocate for my users as I start to design solutions

User Flows

Here I wanted to visualize how to create a flashcard and how to study from a card deck before designing. Mapping out a user flow will prevent me from making too many iterations throughout the design process.

Wireframing

Low Fidelity Wireframes

Here I've created 4 wireframes to represent the actions my users will most likely take once they use the app:

  • Sign up
  • Onboard
  • Create a New Flashcard
  • Study From a Card Deck

Design

Usability Testing

Research Goals: identify points of friction, assess the ease of use, note down emotional state, and note down quotes

Tasks: sign up, onboard, create a flashcard, and study from a card deck

Method & Number: 3 participants, 30 mins each, moderated remote testing via Zoom

Iterations

To my surprise, I uncovered 4 points of friction that were unanimous within my test group. I rated these errors using Jakob Nielsen's scale of severity, and made the following iterations:

Missing Button

When asked to add a new card to the chemistry deck, users went to the card deck first, before going to the navigation at the bottom. This prompted me to add a second button to give users a second location to add a card.

Label Confusion

This was a minor cosmetic fix. Users were unsure what the words "least known" meant. This prompted me to rephrase it to something more obvious. I also added "randomize" to the list of buttons, and got rid of "more options" to reduce the number of taps.

Icon Confusion

When asked to study a deck, two users looked to the navigation bar instead of clicking on a study deck. To correct this, I added a question mark icon at the bottom to represent "quiz".

Card-to-Screen Size

Once the users flipped a flashcard over, they immediately felt stuck due to the feedback rating system taking up 50% of the screen. To correct the heirarchy, I elongated the backside of the flashcard to match the front side.

Conclusion

My keytake away from rapidly prototyping a flashcard app was the importance of user research in the beginning and after creating wireframes. It's best to empathize with the user's pain, and design a solution to meet their needs.

Next Steps

Here is my plan for future iterations:

  • Conduct more qualitative research to back design decisions
  • Move the wireframes up in fidelity
  • Consider adding fun games
  • Design an app logo in Adobe Illustrator
